Social Issues Prevalent in the World Today

Police Brutality

What is Police Brutality?

According to the Law Dictionary, police brutality “is the use of excessive and/or unnecessary force by police when dealing with civilians”. The major debate in media now is the fact that “excessive and/or unnecessary force” is a subjective term and could be interpreted differently between the victim and the officer. Many times there are accusations that police brutality differs by the race and gender of the victim, as African Americans and Middle Eastern men seem to be seeing the worst of the police.

Police brutality has been a problem from the times of the Industrial revolution, and we have had spikes of severe brutality after major attacks, such as that of 9/11.

The Law Dictionary

Recent Events

Though there are dash cams on the police car as well as body cams on the officer themselves, there has been many instances in court where the video has been altered, or the officer him/herself would turn off the camera in order to eradicate evidence.

On August 1st, 2016, there was a case where a Chicago police officer shot and killed an African American 18 year old after he crashed a stolen car. Though spectators saw the event, the police’s body camera was not working during that instance -- something that many people at his court case was suspicious about, as they thought it was just his attempt at a cover up. Eventually, this officer was suspended from his job, but the life of the young man who he killed will never be revived.

New York Times Article

How You Can Help

If you see police brutality wrongly committed, make sure that you take action and make it a point to report the accident and all the witnesses on the scene. The same would apply if you yourself are the victim. Also take pictures of the injuries on the victim and if possible take a video of the police as a way of solid evidence.

Gender Inequality

What is Gender Inequality?

Gender inequality is defined as the imbalance in status between men and women. While women have gained many more freedoms in the last 100, years, some women still experience sexism, even in the United States.

Women have caught up to, and in some countries, even surpass men in terms of education, but discrimination still exists in the workplace. Wage discrimination occurs when equally qualified workers who produce the same amount of work get paid different amounts. Historically speaking, men were paid more than equally qualified women; gender pay gaps persist around the world. According to the International Labour Organization, on average, American women make 81% of their male counterparts earned. Additionally, women made up 24% of CEOs in the United States, but only earned 74.5% as much as male CEOs.

International Labour Organization

Recent Events

Actresses Alysia Reiner and Sarah Morgan Thomas recently founded the company Broad Street Pictures aimed to fight against gender inequality in Hollywood. They want to tell entertaining and though provoking stories about amazing women. "Equity" is their first movie that premiered at this year's Sundance Film Festival. The movie stars Anna Gunn and about an investment banker who is caught in a web of scandal and corruption.

Borad Street Pictures wants to create more opportunities for women to close the gender gap in the film industry by putting more women in front and behind the camera. The company is still in its beginning stages and is being run out Reiner's and Thomas's apartments, but they hope to make big strides for women.

Broad Street Pictures

How You Can Help

In order to help stop gender inequality, it's important to continue to push people to move beyond stereotypes and recognize the contributions that each individual (male or female) can make to the workplace and relationships at home. Encourage young girls to go into STEM fields and other male dominated careers. Support companies that take a strong position on the issue.

Gun Violence

What is Gun Violence?

Current legislation allows Americans to own guns and use them for recreational purposes and to defend against and deter acts of violence Each year tens of thousands of people are injured and killed by firearms. Gun violence occurs most commonly in lower income areas and are frequently associated with gang violence. The media highlights many of the mass shooting incidents, but mass shootings account for only a small fraction of gun related violence. Between 1994 and 2007, the frequency of these incidents declined, but has been rising from 2007 to 2016.

There have been many attempts to reduce gun violence through legislation including restricting the purchase of firearms from youths, establishing stricter background checks, and stiffening the sentencing of gun law violators.

The National Academies Press

Recent Events

On Sunday July 31, a gunman fired into a crowd of people in downtown Austin at 2:15 a.m., killing one woman and leaving four others seriously injured. Police were able to identify the gunman as 24 year old Endicott McCray, but his whereabouts remained unknown. McCray had previously done time in jail on two separate counts for robbery and possession of a firearm. Austin Police Chief Art Acevdeo assured the public that Austin is one of the safest cities in the country despite the incident on Sunday.

Chron Article

What We Can Do

To eliminate gun violence in the United States, we can first make more restrictions on gun sales. Current legislation allows people with criminal records and mental illnesses to purchase guns. Another major step would be to improve the health care system. By improving treatment, we would reduce the chance for people with mental health issues from becoming violent. We could also address the illegal sale of firearms. With new legislation, it would be easier to track guns and where they are coming from. Lastly, more research on gun violence could be conducted. Funding for this research would be able to answer a lot of questions about the recent rise in gun violence that would help stop future gun violence incidents.

Animal Abuse

What is Animal Abuse?

Dog fighting is one of the most heinous forms of animal cruelty. Fighting dogs are typically raised in isolation, so they spend most of their lives on short, heavy chains. They are regularly conditioned for fighting through the use of drugs, including anabolic steroids to enhance muscle mass and encourage aggressiveness. Fighting dogs may have their ears cropped and tails docked close to their bodies to minimize the animal’s normal body language cues and to limit areas that another dog can grab during a fight. Fighters usually perform this cropping/docking themselves using crude and inhumane techniques.

Puppy mills usually house dogs in overcrowded and unsanitary conditions without adequate veterinary care, food, water or socialization. In order to maximize profits, female dogs are bred at every opportunity with little-to-no recovery time between litters. Puppy mill puppies, often as young as eight weeks of age, are sold to pet shops or directly to the public over the Internet, through newspaper ads and at swap meets and flea markets.

ASPCA

Recent Events

As you read this, another innocent animal is being abused, neglected or forced to fight. The first step to creating positive change for these animals is recognizing the cruelty that threatens them.

It has been estimated that there are 900 to 2,000 new cases of animal hoarding every year in the United States, with a quarter of a million animals falling victim. Animals collected range from cats and dogs to reptiles, rodents, birds, exotics and even farm animals

ASPCA Article

What You Can Do

Regardless of where you live, there are many ways for you to help stop animal abuse. The best thing that you can do is report suspicions of animal cruelty to police or animal control agencies. Volunteer at the ASPCA or local organizations. Volunteer opportunities include working in pet adoption, rescue, and rehabilitation centers. Tell your story if you have adopted mistreated animals or helped fight animal cruelty. Lastly, you can donate to local organizations that aim to help stop animal abuse.

Nativism

What is Nativism?

Nativism is the political policy of favoring a group of established citizens of a country over immigrants. In history, there was a large rise in nativism in the 1920s; many new immigrants were moving to the United States, and many Americans feared that new comers would ruin American values and take from their resources. This fear prompted groups like the Ku Klux Klan to emerge and laws that set limitations on immigration to be put into place.

In recent years, the Tea Party has redirected their attention to stopping any laws that would grant illegals in the U.S. citizenship. Republican candidate for the 2016 election, Donald Trump spoke about nativist themes that targeted Muslims and Mexicans.

US History in Context

Recent Events

Former President George W. Bush spoke at a fundraiser for Senator Rob Portman on August 3rd. He attacked policies about isolationism, nativism, and protectionism, which indirectly criticized Donald Trump, Republican nominee. Throughout Trump's presidential campaign, he repeatedly preached xenophobic ideals and said that he would consider supporting NATO allies is they limit immigration from terrorist affected countries.

The Wall Street Journal Article

What You Can Do

Confront xenophobes in your life and talk out their opinions by pointing out their fallacies. It's also important to show people why nativism is problem that is still affecting the United States occur. Nativism often comes from feelings of mistrust in other groups of people. To break these stereotypes, it's important to ask why different groups are seen as threatening. Even if nativism isn't directly effecting you, other groups need to be considered. Voting for political candidates who support the equality of people regardless of their backgrounds. Lastly, you can encourage laws that combat xenophobia and allow the country to advance towards a more equal society.
